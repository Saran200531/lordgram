1. App Overview & Objectives 
Name (placeholder): [Your App Name] 
Purpose: Enable friends to share images, videos, and reels while fostering real-world 
connections through meetups and intentional interactions. 
Core Objectives: 
 Prioritize authentic connections with people users already know. 
 Turn shared content into opportunities for interaction, discussion, and real-life meetups. 
 Offer optional public discovery for content without making it the primary focus. 
 Provide a mobile-first experience with web accessibility. 
Success Metrics (Day 1 focus): 
 Users can share content easily with friends. 
 Users can create or coordinate meetups from shared posts. 
 Users feel less social pressure and more authentic engagement than on existing platforms. 
2. Target Audience 
Primary Users: 
 Individuals who want to share moments with friends. 
 Users who enjoy posting and viewing short-form content (images, videos, reels). 
 People seeking real-world interaction sparked by online sharing. 
Secondary Users: 
 Creators or friends with global visibility who want optional public discovery. 
 Users who consume content passively but may engage through shared posts. 
Demographics & Context: 
 Age: 16–35 (core early adopters) 
 Context: Mobile-first usage during daily life, web usage for planning or browsing 
 Behavior: Users value authenticity, intentional sharing, and real-world connection

4. User Interface Design Flows 
1. Home / Feed 
 Shows posts from friends primarily, optional public posts second. 
 Each post shows content, reactions, comments, and “share with friend / plan meetup” 
buttons. 
2. Content Creation 
 Upload image/video/reel 
 Add optional caption, tag friends 
 Choose visibility: Friends only / Public 
3. Meetup / Interaction 
 Tap “plan meetup” on a post 
 Select friends, choose time/location 
 Send invites and track RSVPs 
4. Profile 
 Shows user’s posts, friends list, and optional bio 
 Settings for notifications and privacy 
5. Notifications 
 Alerts for comments, invites, and shared posts 
 Clear distinction between friend-focused and public activity 
5. Security Considerations 
 User authentication and authorization for friends-only content 
 Privacy controls for public vs. private posts 
 Safe handling of multimedia content 
 Simple reporting/blocking for inappropriate content
